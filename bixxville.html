03  —  Bixxville

The OS underneath
everything we run.

Bixxville is the operating system Land of Geb runs on — one set of rules for scoping work, proving it happened, and paying for it, no matter what the work is.

Every engagement, in any market, moves through the same four mechanics below. Learn these once and you understand how any project on the network gets scoped, run, and paid.

The mechanics

01 — Sizing

LOGs

The base unit of work. One LOG is one hour. Every task, in every market, gets sized in LOGs before it starts — so effort is comparable across completely different kinds of work.

1 LOG = 1 hour
02 — Pricing

The price follows the work

Price isn't negotiated up front — it's derived. Each LOG is priced at the operator's tier rate, and the total is the sum across every scoped task.

price = Σ (LOGs × tier rate)
03 — Reputation

Proven, not claimed

Every operator carries a reputation score built from real outcomes — did the work land, was it on time, is there a track record, were there disputes. Work routes to proven capability, not to whoever asked first.

R = 0.50·Delivery + 0.25·Timeliness + 0.15·Streak − 0.10·Disputes
04 — Settlement

Scoped, verified, settled

Work isn't paid on a promise. Each unit is confirmed as a real, checkable outcome before it settles — so payment follows proof, not a timesheet and an argument.

How a project actually moves

01Bring a goal.You describe what you're trying to get done and what "done" looks like.
02It gets decomposed.The goal breaks into scoped units of work, each sized in LOGs.
03It's mapped as a graph.The order that actually finishes it — what runs in parallel, what's blocked — replaces a guessed-at list.
04Work routes to proven operators.Each unit goes to whoever on the network has earned the reputation to run it.
05It settles on proof.Each unit is verified as done, then paid — scoped, verified, settled.
