01  —  Decomposition

A goal is never one thing.

Every engagement breaks into scoped units of work. One LOG is one hour. When the parts are named, sized, and priced, the whole stops being a guess — and the order stops being a coin flip.

02  —  The Graph

Lists lie.
A graph tells the truth.

A list pretends work is a straight line. It is not. A graph shows what runs in parallel, what is blocked, and the hidden rule that saves the whole thing — confirm the room before you build the deck.

03  —  How we price

Most businesses price the work
before they understand it.

We do it the other way.

Before we name a number, we map the work. Every task has a size. Every hour of attention has a value. The price is the output of that equation — not a guess, not a market rate we hoped would fit, not a number designed to sound reasonable. You see the math before you sign anything. If it fits, you know exactly what you're buying. If it doesn't, that's a real answer too.

The number follows the work. Always.
